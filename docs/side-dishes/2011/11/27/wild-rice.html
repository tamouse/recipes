Wild Rice 27-Nov-2011

Categories: side-dishes Tags:

Wild rice is a nutty, dense version of rice, delicious addition to casseroles and soups.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup uncooked wild rice
  • 3 cups water

Directions

  1. Wash 1 cup uncooked wild rice thoroughly.

  2. In a pot, bring 3 cups lightly salted water to a boil.

  3. Add the 1 cup wild rice to the boiling water, returning water to a boil, and stir.

  4. Reduce heat and simmer, covered, 50-60 minutes or until the kernels pop.

  5. Fluff with a fork, and simmer an additional 5 to 10 minutes uncovered.

  6. Drain any excess fluid.

For additional flavour, cook in chicken or beef broth. Note: avoid bouillon as it is far too salty.

You can cook wild rice in the oven instead of the stove top; it can be put directly into casseroles that will bake for a long time, provided there is enough moisture in the casserole.
